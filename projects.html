Micromouse

Micromouse is a robotics competition where teams build and program a robot to solve a maze. These robots compete to solve the maze the fastest. Micrmouse is a beginner friendly project as students learn the basics of working with electronics and microcontrollers.

UCR Unmanned Aerial Systems' mission is to foster a professional environment that inspires innovative and practical solutions in autonomous technologies. Specializing in the design, development, and deployment of Unmanned Aerial Systems, our multidisciplinary team serves to inspire, educate, and innovate these solutions in our community while competing in annual competitions.

This year, UCR-UAS is pursuing the development of our system�s full-scale prototype, the Borealis, by the end of the 2020-2021 academic year. This prototype is a precursor towards our competition solution, the Aurora, which is capable of object recognition, autonomous flight, obstacle avoidance, payload delivery, and mapping. This project is driven by the collective efforts of over 50 undergraduate students, each specializing in one of our eight sub-teams. The Borealis is scheduled to be presented at the UCR Aerospace Conference this coming Spring Quarter.
